I study how Diffusion Models and Normalizing Flows learn from finite data resources. I also have a background in statistical physics and have worked on problems in network science and the spread of disease. A full list of my publications can be found [here](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=rWAQ9IgAAAAJ&hl=en). Below is a selection of recent work.

## Research output (preprints & published work)
- [Generalization Dynamics of Linear Diffusion models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.24769), Claudia Merger & Sebastian Goldt. We investigate how the covariance statistics typically found in image data affect learning in diffusion models.
- [A theory of learning data statistics in diffusion models, from easy to hard](https://openreview.net/forum?id=2gntRWnqbP), 
  Lorenzo Bardone, Claudia Merger, Sebastian Goldt. We demonstrate that diffusion models sequentially learn first simpler, then more complicated statistics and predict at which sample complexity statistics of different orders are learned
- [Learning Interacting Theories from Data](https://journals.aps.org/prx/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevX.13.041033), Claudia Merger, Alexandre René, et al., Phys. Rev. X 13, 041033. We developed a new method for statistical inference using a trained generative AI model (a Normalizing Flow) and empirically demonstrated that in the case of insufficient model capacity, Normalizing Flows learn effective statistics of a dataset based on lower order cumulants.
